Why Now
Three structural forces are converging to create a breakout opportunity in finish materials — a $400B+ market that has never had a credible digital-native brand. Two months of real operating data confirms the thesis.
📊
Total Addressable Market
The US finish materials market — tile, stone, flooring, countertops — exceeds $400B annually. Dominated by big-box retail and fragmented local distributors, none of which have built a compelling digital experience. There is no Warby Parker of finish materials.
$400B+
🔥
LA Fires: Once-in-a-Generation Demand
The January 2025 LA wildfires destroyed 12,000+ structures and caused an estimated $135B+ in damages. The rebuild cycle for finish materials will play out over 3–5 years. Reno Source is positioned to serve this surge in demand precisely when local supply chains are overwhelmed.
12,000+ Homes
🏠
Aging US Housing Stock
The median US home is now over 40 years old. More than 40 million homes were built before 1980 — and the tile, flooring, and countertops in those homes are long overdue for replacement. This is a structural, multi-decade tailwind that creates persistent renovation demand regardless of market cycles.

The Long Game
The 4 Pillars of Shareholder Value Creation
01
Customer Obsession
Tight feedback loops between what homeowners want and what we stock, price, and ship. When the customer drives every decision, the value compounds. 700+ sample kit orders in M1 alone is the proof point.
02
Category Expansion
Every new category increases order value and strengthens the flywheel. More product, more reasons to buy. The platform gets more valuable with every category we add.
03
Technology
We use tech to stay lean and build a shopping experience that actually helps homeowners make decisions. Better systems mean lower overhead, faster shipping, and more margin to reinvest in product and price.
04
Logistics
Fast shipping and easy returns. The quicker product gets to the customer and the simpler we make it to send back, the more confidence people have buying finish materials online.

Actual Results (M1 & M2)

Month 1 (April 2026) and Month 2 (May 2026) figures reflected in this model are unaudited management estimates based on data from Shopify, Triple Whale, and internal records. These figures are directionally accurate but have not been audited or formally reconciled and should not be relied upon as final financial statements.

Model Methodology

This pro forma model reflects two months of actual operating results (M1: April 2026, M2: May 2026) and 22 months of forward-looking projections (M3–M24). Revenue targets are held constant at management's base projections. CPC curve: $0.70/click (M1 actual) rising to $1.80/click by M24, reflecting improving retargeting efficiency and creative performance as the brand scales. Gross margin begins at 48–50% in early months (reflecting actual product mix) and stabilizes at 45% by M10 as scale increases fulfillment costs.

Working Capital

Model assumes access to working capital for inventory replenishment beyond the initial raise. Inventory expansion beyond the existing position may require additional financing.
