Why Now
Three structural forces are converging to create a breakout opportunity in finish materials — a $400B+ market that has never had a credible digital-native brand.
📊
Total Addressable Market
The US finish materials market — tile, stone, flooring, countertops — exceeds $400B annually. It is dominated by big-box retail and fragmented local distributors, none of which have built a compelling digital experience. There is no Warby Parker of finish materials. No digitally-native brand owns this space. That gap is the opportunity.
$400B+
🔥
LA Fires: Once-in-a-Generation Demand
The January 2025 LA wildfires destroyed 12,000+ structures and caused an estimated $135B+ in damages — one of the most destructive wildfire events in US history. The rebuild cycle for finish materials will play out over 3–5 years. Reno Source is positioned to serve this surge in demand precisely when local supply chains are overwhelmed and homeowners are turning online.
12,000+ Homes
🏠
Aging US Housing Stock
The median US home is now over 40 years old. More than 40 million homes were built before 1980 — and the tile, flooring, and countertops in those homes are long overdue for replacement. This isn't a trend. It's a structural, multi-decade tailwind that creates persistent renovation demand regardless of interest rates or market cycles.
40M+ Homes

Model Methodology

This pro forma model reflects two months of actual operating results (M1: April 2026, M2: May 2026) and 22 months of forward-looking projections (M3–M24). Revenue targets are held constant at management's base projections. CPC curve: $0.70/click (M1 actual) rising to $1.80/click by M24, reflecting improving retargeting efficiency and creative performance as the brand scales. Gross margin begins at 48–50% in actuals and stabilizes at 45% by M10+ at scale.

Working Capital

Model assumes access to working capital for inventory replenishment beyond the initial raise. Inventory expansion beyond the existing $1.9M landed cost position may require additional financing.
